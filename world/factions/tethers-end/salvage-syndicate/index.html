    Salvage Syndicate¶

    Tether's End's smugglers' guild — such as it is. The Syndicate operates lift gangs at the Grand Anchor, runs informal cargo routes that the Riftwatch politely doesn't notice, and brokers goods that fall outside the Court's sanctioned trade. Officially they do not exist as a recognized organization. Practically they are the single largest employer in Tether's End and the working logistics layer the village runs on.

    What they do¶

    • Lift work. Anchor crews pulling crates, cargo, and salvage off the ledges below the village. The official-looking work; mostly legitimate freight handling.
    • Quiet runs. Move goods between Tether's End and the surface in defiance of the descent prohibition. Most cargo is mundane (leathers, ores, herbs that grow only below); most of the village pretends not to know.
    • Brokerage. Match buyers and sellers across the legitimacy line. A noble House quietly wants something Tether's End can supply; the Syndicate finds a willing supplier and takes a cut. The brokerage office sits above the Salt Wharf.
    • Protection. The Syndicate has its own enforcers. Disputes inside Tether's End — debt, theft, broken contracts — usually settle through the Syndicate before they reach the Riftwatch.

    Structure¶

    There is no formal hierarchy. There is:

    • The Boss. A single informal title held by acclamation; currently Cassio Underbrook, a halfling who has been at Tether's End for thirty years and Boss for the last twelve. The title is held until the holder loses the room.
    • Crew bosses. Five. One each for lift work, quiet runs, brokerage, enforcement, and the Salt Wharf cargo flow.
    • Crew members. Roughly eighty active across the five crews.
    • The Boss's table. Six chairs at the back of the Anchor and Lantern (the tavern the Syndicate informally owns). Cassio holds court there three evenings a week.

    Public figures¶

    • Boss Cassio Underbrook. Pragmatic, well-liked, soft-spoken. The Syndicate's senior figure for twelve years.
    • Lift Gang Boss Korbin Stoneveil. The most publicly visible Syndicate figure to outsiders; runs the official-looking lift work.
    • Brokerage Manager Lethaiyn Greenways. Runs the office above the Salt Wharf. The Syndicate's polished face; can pass as a legitimate factor in any of the five capitals.

    Reputation¶

    Outside Tether's End: criminal, dangerous, beneath notice. Inside Tether's End: the people you actually pay rent to. The Syndicate is more orderly than its outsider reputation suggests — partly because chaos is bad for trade, and partly because the Anchor itself is a place where carelessness gets people killed.
