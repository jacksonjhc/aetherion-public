    Cogbarrow¶

    Capital of Glymswick, the gnomes' great city, seated over the Crowngear on the continent's eastern reach. If Aurion is where Aetherion is ruled and Khalvar Hold is where it is held up, Cogbarrow is where it is steered: the city that sits atop the vast mechanism by which the gnomes angle the whole drifting continent against the wind. It is the loudest, brightest, most inventive place on Aetherion — and the only city where a stranger's worth is read off a number stamped on a brass plate.

    Cogbarrow, ringed around the Crowngear — the inner city turning slowly over the great mechanism that steers the continent.

    The shape of the city — the Turning City¶

    Where Aurion climbs a plateau and a person's station is how high they live, and Khalvar Hold is cut downward and a dwarf's standing is how deep she's trusted to go, Cogbarrow turns inward — and a gnome's standing is how far in toward the Gear their work is licensed to sit. The city is built as a great set of concentric rings around the central Works that drops to the Crowngear. The outer rings are fixed ground, and anyone's to walk. The inner rings are the turning ones — they revolve, slowly and ceaselessly, over the buried Gear — and to set your workbench on them you must earn the right by patent, license, and proven invention.

    This makes Cogbarrow the one city sorted by paper. "What's your number?" is the question here, and the number is the one stamped on the brass plate every licensed gnome wears: it says how far in you're cleared to work, and so it says who you are. You don't climb your way up or earn your way down in Cogbarrow. You file your way in.

    Five rings, outer to inner:

    • The Rimworks — the fixed-ground edge: the unlicensed tinkerers' sheds, the immigrant quarters, and the great Convention fairground. Anyone may work here; no plate required. Where every gnomish career, and most gnomish inventions, begin.
    • The Manufactories — the working city: the licensed workshops, the foundries, the markets, and the homes of most of Cogbarrow, with the transfer-yards along its inner edge where the turning city is boarded.
    • The Brass Rings — the credentialed heart, where the Turning begins: the master-workshops and guild-halls of the Engineer-Wrights, and the Patent Office that gates the whole inner city.
    • The Gearhead — the innermost, fastest-turning ring: the seat of the Gearwright's Consortium and its Senior Council, crown of the Works.
    • The Underworks — below the Works-shaft: the Crowngear itself, tended by the deep-licensed and seen by almost no one.

    The Turning City: fixed rings without, turning rings within, the Crowngear at the heart — and the Last Gate Mausoleum standing apart, the one great thing that does not turn.

    Getting around¶

    Cogbarrow gates with paperwork. Where Aurion checks you at a guarded gate on every ring and Khalvar at every band, here the gate is the brass plate stamped with your patent-number and license-grade. The outer rings are open to all; to board the turning rings you show your plate at the transfer-thresholds, and a plate that doesn't clear the grade simply isn't waved across. The true gate of the city is the Patent Office, where a number is granted, raised, or revoked.

    You board the inner rings at transfer-bridges — platforms where a fixed gallery meets a moving one and you step across at the moment they align, the way a gnome child learns to step onto a slow carousel. Conveyor-walks and counterweighted lifts carry the rest, all of them keeping time to the Turn. A Cogbarrow gnome reads the hour, the shift, and the date off the alignment of the turning rings against the fixed city, without ever glancing at a watch — and can tell an outsider at once by the half-beat they're always behind.

    The Turning¶

    Cogbarrow's marvel is that its heart turns. The inner city revolves — slowly, ponderously, ceaselessly — once a day over the buried Crowngear, slow enough to live on and vast enough that the rings nearest the center sweep visibly past the fixed ground at the rim. With it comes a deep, slow pulse, one beat as each great tooth of the Gear engages, felt in the floor and the breastbone. The gnomes revere it: it is the sign that the Gear runs, that the continent is steered, that the work goes on. "The Gear turns" is how a Cogbarrow gnome says all is well — and in living memory it has never once stopped.

    The Turning — the inner rings revolving over the Crowngear, the pulse of the Gear felt through the whole city.

    What Cogbarrow is known for¶

    • The Crowngear. The continent's drift-control mechanism, deep beneath the city, by which the gnomes angle Aetherion's heading against the prevailing winds. Without it, the continent goes where the wind puts it. Only the highest engineers know its full workings.
    • The Turning City. The only city on Aetherion whose heart revolves — a feat of gnomish engineering unmatched anywhere, and the source of the city's whole sense of time.
    • The patent system. Continent-wide intellectual-property law is a Glymswick invention, and the Patent Office is its temple. The Consortium owns most of the patents that matter, and the brass plate is the gnome's name, rank, and permit in one.
    • The Last Gate Mausoleum. The continent's great burying-place, drawing pilgrims and grieving heirs from all five nations — and the one great structure in Cogbarrow that deliberately does not turn.
    • The Convention. The Consortium's annual gathering of Engineer-Wrights, held on the Rimworks fairground in early spring, when Cogbarrow is busiest and an unknown tinkerer can change their life in three days.

    Living here¶

    • Speech: Common, with a heavy load of technical and craft loanwords; the gnomish craft-cant is what you learn if you mean to rise. Naming is craft-suffix — Steamspoke, Twogear, Pendulum, Glasswork — marking a family's original trade.
    • Aesthetic: brass, glass, lamp-light, polished wood. Long aprons, goggles pushed up on foreheads, ink-stained sleeves, and the brass plate at every belt and door.
    • Manners: Cogbarrow respects proven work above blood, charm, or coin. Shop-talk is the real conversation, the plate is read before the face, and the surest way to earn a gnome's regard is to follow the cant and build something that holds.
