    Aurion¶

    Capital of Highcrown, and the capital of the whole flying continent — the seat of the Majestal Court, the home of the gods-in-residence, and the destination every pilgrim on Aetherion hopes to climb at least once.

    If you are reading this, you probably live here. This is your city.

    Aurion, capital of Highcrown — and of the continent.

    One city on a whole floating world: Aurion crowns the high heart of the continent, with forests, farmlands, mountains, and the other realms spread below.

    The shape of the city¶

    Aurion is built up the sides of the highest plateau on the continent, in great rings that climb to the summit. The higher you go, the grander it gets — and the harder it is to get there. Five rings, from the top down:

    • The Crown — the summit. The Majestal Palace, the great temples of the Twelve, and the Mirror of the Twelve, the shining reservoir that crowns the city. Where the Court sits and the gods keep their seats.
    • The Coronet — the noble ring. The terraced estates and water-gardens of the fifty-three Houses, the Crown Assembly's hall, and the Solnaris Opera House. Marble, gold leaf, and old family pride.
    • The mid-quarter (formally the Burgage Tiers) — the working heart of the city. The Bank of Aurion, the guild-streets, the craftsfolk and merchants, the markets. Most of the city's real business happens here.
    • The Low Quarters — the base of the plateau, ringed by the canal and its docks. Tenements, brewers, fishmongers, watermen. Poor, crowded, and the friendliest part of the city if it likes you.
    • The Spillways — the drains and sluices below the Low Quarters, where the canal pours off the plateau's edge. Nobody much goes down there.

    The rings of Aurion, with the Sacred Way climbing from the docks to the Crown.

    The rings, seen¶

    The Crown: the Mirror of the Twelve and the great temples at the summit.

    The Coronet: the terraced estates and water-gardens of the noble Houses.

    The mid-quarter: the guild-streets and markets, the golden summit far above.

    The Low Quarters: the canal and its docks at the foot of the city.

    Getting around¶

    Each ring is walled off from the one above by a gate-stair, kept by the Skyguard. Passing upward takes a reason — the right dress, the right errand, or a gate-token stamped for the tier you're climbing to. Most folk live and work their whole lives without setting foot above the mid-quarter.

    The one road open to everyone is the Sacred Way, the great processional avenue that switchbacks all the way from the pilgrim-gates at the base to the temples at the Crown. Cargo and water are hauled up the tiers on gnomish water-lifts and barge-locks; nobles ride enclosed chair-funiculars; everyone else takes the stairs.

    The Sacred Way — the one road open to all, climbing from the base to the Crown.

    A tier-gate: the guarded stair between one ring and the next. You climb only with reason to.

    The Descent — the water of the gods¶

    Aurion's marvel is its water. At the summit, the Mirror of the Twelve is fed by engines that draw moisture from the high winds the continent rides, and from the Mirror the water cascades down through every ring of the city — through the nobles' water-gardens, into the public fountains and canals, down at last to the Low Quarters and the docks. The faithful call it the Descent and drink it as a blessing: the gods' gift, flowing down to all.

    You can tell how high up the city you are with your eyes shut. The Crown is cool and misted and clean; the Low Quarters smell of canal-smoke, brewing mash, and fish.

    The pilgrim city¶

    As the seat of the pantheon, Aurion is where the faithful of all five nations come. The Calendar of the Twelve rules the city's year, and its great festivals bring pilgrim tides that pack the inns, fill the alms-houses, and crowd the Sacred Way with relic-sellers, preachers, and the devout. For a few weeks at a stretch the whole city swells, and then empties again.

    A festival night: the whole city strung with lanterns, the temples floodlit above, sky-lanterns rising.

    What Aurion is known for¶

    • The Court. The Majestal Court is the most powerful institution on the continent — sermons, festivals, the calendar, the courts, all flow from the Crown.
    • The gods-in-residence. More of the Twelve keep their seats in Aurion than anywhere else. Majesty presides from the Palace at the Crown. Mercy keeps the great Aurelium Hospice, whose doors open onto the Low Quarters so the poor and the dying can reach her without climbing. Muse composes for the Solnaris Opera House on the Coronet.
    • The Inquisition and the Pure Flame. The Court's enforcement arms. The Skyward Inquisition's fortress, the Pyre Bastion, looks down over the working tiers — a reminder, never far from view, of who keeps the faith.
    • The Bank of Aurion. Mints the faith coin and holds most of the noble Houses' debts. Quietly behind everything.

    Living here¶

    • Speech: Common, in the formal Aurion register. How you talk marks which tier you're from.
    • Faith: the calendar runs the day. Bells mark the hours; rest-day empties the workshops; scripture is quoted at conversational volume.
    • Rank: in Aurion, your station is something you can see — it's how high up the city you live, and climbing it is the work of a lifetime.
